Activity

The Administrative Litigation refers to the set of judicial processes involving Public Administration and its acts. This branch of law aims to ensure that administrative acts, such as decisions, regulations, or contracts, are executed according to the law, protecting citizens’ rights against possible abuses of power by the State. Through administrative litigation, individuals can challenge the legality of administrative acts and seek compensation for damages caused by public authority actions.

This type of litigation involves the review of administrative decisions directly affecting citizens’ interests, such as tax issues, licenses, or permits. When a person or company believes their rights have been harmed by an administrative act, they may appeal to the administrative court to seek review or annulment of that decision. Administrative litigation is therefore a crucial tool to ensure transparency, legality, and justice in State actions.

The Tax and Fiscal Litigation deals with legal disputes between taxpayers and the State related to the application of tax laws. This branch of law involves resolving conflicts about tax payment obligations, interpreting tax regulations, and challenging tax assessments and penalties imposed by tax authorities. The focus is to ensure taxation is fair and that tax obligations are met according to current legislation.

This field includes defending taxpayers’ rights in cases of undue collection, tax disputes, and other fiscal measures, as well as regulating the State’s role in tax inspection and collection. The goal is to ensure a balance between fulfilling tax obligations and protecting taxpayers’ rights within the tax system.

The Commercial and Corporate Law regulates economic and business activities, ensuring a stable legal framework for the creation, management, and dissolution of companies. This branch of law covers the rules governing relations between merchants, companies, and other economic agents, promoting market security and transparency.

Fundamental to the operation of businesses, Commercial and Corporate Law defines rules for company formation, commercial contracts, liability of partners and directors, as well as mechanisms for resolving business disputes. Its application aims to ensure balance in commercial relations and foster a solid and competitive business environment.

Within this area, we operate in the following issues:

  • Commercial contracts

The Family Law is the branch of law that regulates legal relationships between family members, addressing essential issues such as marriage, common-law partnerships, parentage, child custody, adoption, and inheritance. This field of law aims to protect the individual and collective rights of family members, promoting harmonious coexistence and the well-being of those involved. Legislation in this area also deals with the protection of children, the elderly, and other vulnerable family members.

Furthermore, Family Law deals with matters such as marital property regimes, division of assets, alimony, and the definition of parental responsibilities. The goal is to resolve family disputes in a balanced and fair manner, ensuring that each individual's rights are respected and that the best interests of the children are always prioritized in judicial decisions.

Within this area, we handle the following issues:

  • Paternity and Child Support Actions
  • Separation of Property and Separation of Persons and Property Actions
  • Adoption
  • Annulment of Catholic Marriage
  • Disinheritance
  • Divorce by Mutual Consent and Divorce without Consent of the Other Spouse
  • Family Mediation
  • Protection of Children and Youth at Risk
  • Parental Responsibilities

The Inheritance Law / Inventory deals with the transfer of assets, rights, and obligations of a deceased person to their heirs. This branch of law covers the rules regarding testamentary and intestate succession, that is, the distribution of assets according to the will or, in its absence, according to legal provisions. Inheritance Law also addresses the heirs' responsibility for the deceased’s debts and the management of the estate.

Inventory is a legal procedure aimed at identifying and valuing the assets left by the deceased, as well as determining the rights and obligations of the heirs. This process is essential to formalize the division of assets and can be conducted judicially or extrajudicially, depending on the case complexity and the heirs’ agreement. Through inventory, it is ensured that succession is carried out fairly and according to current legislation, avoiding conflicts among those involved.

Within this area, we handle the following matters:

  • Judicial and Extrajudicial Division of Assets
  • Wills, exchanges, and donations

The Consumer Law is a branch of law that protects consumers’ interests in their relationships with providers of goods and services. This field covers various issues such as protection against unfair commercial practices, misleading advertising, and abusive contractual clauses. The main objective is to ensure consumers have access to clear information, that their rights are respected, and that they can seek compensation for damages caused by illegal or harmful practices.

In the legal context, consumer law aims to balance the relationship between consumers and businesses, recognizing the vulnerability of consumers and ensuring their protection against practices that may harm their physical, moral, or financial integrity. This includes the right to product returns, damage repairs, and compensation for losses suffered, always based on legislation regulating consumer relations.

Within this area, we handle the following matters:

  • Warranties

The Civil Law is the foundation of the legal system and regulates relationships between private individuals, ensuring rights and duties in everyday society. This branch of law covers various areas including contractual obligations, civil liability, property rights, family relations, and inheritance.

Essential to guaranteeing justice in interactions between individuals, Civil Law establishes the principles governing contracts, possession and ownership of property, as well as rights and duties in family relationships. Its application aims to promote legal certainty, protect citizens' interests, and ensure fair resolution of private conflicts.

Within this area, we work on the following issues:

  • Civil Actions
  • Family and Minors
  • Obligations and Contracts
  • Condominium Property
  • Inheritance and Wills
  • Judicial Courts

Labor Law and Litigation regulates labor relations between employers and employees, ensuring a balance between the rights and duties of both parties. This area of law covers the formation, execution, and termination of employment contracts, protection of workers' rights, as well as regulation of working conditions, workplace safety, and health.

Within labor litigation, it deals with the resolution of conflicts arising from employment relationships, whether through negotiation, administrative or judicial means. Issues such as dismissals, workplace harassment, occupational accidents, breaches of contractual terms, and wage claims are frequently subject to litigation requiring specialized legal intervention to protect the interests of those involved.

Within this area, we handle the following issues:

  • Contracts
  • Employment relationship

Criminal Law and Litigation is the branch of law that regulates criminal offenses and the sanctions applicable to individuals who commit crimes. This field of law defines what constitutes a crime, the procedures for investigation, prosecution, and trial, and the penalties that can be imposed on offenders. Criminal litigation specifically refers to judicial disputes related to crimes, where the State, through the Public Prosecutor's Office, prosecutes individuals who break the law.

The objective of Criminal Law is to ensure social order, preventing behaviors that may harm the community, punishing those responsible, and rehabilitating offenders whenever possible. In a criminal process, the defendant has constitutional rights to a fair and impartial trial, and the defense attorney plays a crucial role in protecting these rights, being able to challenge evidence, question allegations, and plead for acquittal or sentence reduction.

Within this area, we work on the following issues:

  • Administrative Offenses
  • Criminal

Enforcement Law is the area of law that regulates processes aimed at the coercive collection of obligations, such as debts, contract enforcement, and execution of judicial decisions. Through available legal means, it aims to ensure that creditors obtain satisfaction of their claims when debtors do not voluntarily fulfill their obligations.

This branch of law includes mechanisms such as seizures, attachments, mortgage executions, and other coercive measures, ensuring that creditors can recover amounts owed fairly and effectively, always respecting the fundamental rights of the parties involved.

Within this area, we work on the following issues:

  • Debt Collection Letters
  • Initiation of Enforcement Proceedings
  • Injunctive Proceedings
  • Others

Real Estate Law is the branch of law that regulates legal relationships related to real estate, including purchase, sale, leasing, construction, use, and management of properties. This field covers both property rights and real rights over real estate, as well as issues related to possession and use. It also deals with the drafting and execution of real estate contracts such as sales, leases, exchanges, among others.

Additionally, Real Estate Law addresses matters such as property regularization, obtaining licenses and permits, and resolving disputes arising from land issues. This branch also involves contract analysis and conflict resolution between buyers, sellers, landlords, and tenants, always aiming to guarantee legal security and compliance with applicable laws in the real estate sector.

Road and Traffic Law and Litigation regulates road circulation and safety rules, as well as the resolution of disputes arising from traffic offenses and road accidents. This branch aims to ensure the proper application of traffic rules, protect the rights of drivers, pedestrians, and other road users, and guarantee civil and criminal liability in case of infractions.

Furthermore, it includes the defense of drivers in administrative and judicial proceedings, contestation of traffic fines, liability claims for damages resulting from road accidents, and matters related to car insurance. Its objective is to promote safety on public roads and ensure fair resolution of conflicts within the traffic scope.

Within this area, we work on the following issues:

  • Driver’s license
  • Vehicle registration
  • Traffic and contestation of fines

Notary and Registries is an area of law that ensures the authenticity, validity, and publicity of legal acts, guaranteeing their legality and legal security. The notarial activity plays an essential role in formalizing contracts, deeds, wills, and other acts requiring public faith.

The registry system, in turn, ensures the registration and publicity of relevant legal facts, such as civil registration (births, marriages, deaths), property registration (transfers and encumbrances on real estate), and commercial registration (company formation and amendments). This area of law is fundamental to guarantee transparency, security, and legal effectiveness in patrimonial and business relations.

Within this area, we work on the following issues:

  • Document Authentication
  • Residence Authorization
  • Nationality
  • Signature Recognition
  • Registries
  • Company formation/dissolution
